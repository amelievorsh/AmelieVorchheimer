Amelie Vorchheimer

BIO

Amelie is a queer multimedia artist working in Naarm Australia who works with video, installation, sound, and new media art to explore non-violent forms of changemaking in response to identity politics online.

Due to finish their Bachelor of Fine Arts in January 2026, they are focused on community-based practice, running the RMIT drawing club and hosting a number of DIY exhibitions. Their research unpacks the impacts and potential responses to the attention economy, unpacking the rhizomatic nature of data mining practices, neurodegeneration, and ability to critically engage.

Amelie’s practice dissects current uses and interactions with online spaces, and how this impacts interpersonal relationships. Their practice aims to create a space that doubles as a site for inclusive engagement and play, fostering critical engagement and reclamation of digital spaces.
