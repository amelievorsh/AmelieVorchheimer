What it it that it is

What it it that it is

What is it that is was a multi phase social constructivism project that explored cognitive development techniques in response to neurodeterioration as a result of the attention economy. It was positioned as a research project, that aimed to create community adhesion. There were 5 main iterations, Professional business meeting, Happening #1, What is it that it is, Mediated Brainstorm and A fragment. Each was conducted in a different location with a different group of people. The intention was to connect people from different demographic through different means of communication such as drawing, prompt cards, music and interactive work. This project is ongoing throughout my practice as I seek to unpack the impacts of vocabulary and social skill loss as a result of internet use.

Visualised Screaming, 2024

Visualised Screaming, 2024

Visualised Screaming emerged from the data and recordings collected in What is it that it is. Using a spectrum analyser, I translated spoken audio from the discussions into visual forms. This transformation critiqued algorithmic systems that continuously analyse human behaviour for consumption. By creating a "redundant" form of analysis, the work questions how digital systems absorb and commodify emotion. Images show work presented in a range of spaces inc. miscellania melbourne.

I have Detained your attention, 2025

I have Detained your attention, 2025

This ongoing project extends my investigation into human–machine dynamics. Presented as an immersive installation, it combines sound, interactive media, floor decals, and a website to question surveillance and autonomy. Within this environment, the machine assumes control, dictating how audiences move and listen. The installation draws on social constructivism, liminality, and intersectional feminist theory to consider how data and behaviour intersect in algorithmic spaces.

Part of the work includes Algorithmic Meditations, a code-based sound generator where code itself performs agency. The audience becomes the instrument, translating generative audio into physical gestures.

From Glen Waverly

From Glen Waverly

These are a series of videos that are made from phone recordings out the window of a train. I attempted to induce a feeling of anxiety and dissociation. These feelings are often associated with my personal experience of disengagement with an experience as dictated by digital worlds. I started using the method of "encryption". The purpose of applying it to this practice is to mirror the experience of complete loss of context and understanding that comes with dissociation through technology.

This work has been displayed as Part of the RMIT IDAHOBIT Exhibition 2025, Fozza Presents 2025, Cross Pollination 2025, and POP POP, 2025.

A fragment, 2024

A fragment, 2024

A fragment, 2024, was an art exhibition and music event curated in collaboration with Hannah Ogawa. Our intention was to create a site of refusal that sat within the "third space" as a means of rebuilding predefined structures through refusal. The event featured 14 artists and 5 DJs, with a mixture of digital and physical installation works, and an iteration of What is it that it is (2024). Images show installation.

Watching the watcher 2025

Watching the watcher 2025

In response to my iterative project last year, What is it that it is (2024 - ongoing), which explored methods for engaging participants in critical discourse and community building, I created a work that explored the underlying themes that inspired the work. Data mining and attention capitalism dictates the spaces we use online, harvesting our time and attention through systems of analysis that were developed to form algorithms. I created my own system of analysis using the recordings from my previous project, by placing them through phase analysis and then attempting to trace this image in real time. To create the sound that was analysed, I layered them over themselves until they were unable to be heard, thus 'encrypting' the information so that only I knew it's true meaning. The work addresses surveillance of information online and how we are constantly survielled even when we feel we are surveilling.

The work places the audience in a position of analyser, whilst inhibiting them from understanding the contexts of the content. This aims to be a replication of the way that information is communicated online.

Images capture stills from the work and documentation of it exhibited Intersections, 2025.

Cheap Solutions 2025

Cheap Solutions 2025

When the environment fails to regrow, where does that leave us?

Donna Haraway argues "the cyborg as a fiction mapping our social and bodily reality", suggesting that due to our reliance and compliance with technological realities, we have become cyborgian creatures. This consumer culture, existing to numb us, removes us from the reality that is the acceleration of the destruction of Earth. Online, the information we consume has the potential to teach us, whether real or false information. Drawing on a culture built on information economies, this work questions you.

What do you do with the information provided to you?

How do you reclaim autonomy from machines that exist to spread misinformation?

Images document the work exhibited as part of Decay, 2025.

Untitled Collab with Lillah S-D

Untitled Collab with Lillah S-D

In collaboration with Lillah Summers Dixon, I created Silenced, and Unitled. These works explored the role of men in the public eye, and how these spaces are dominated by upper class, "professionals". Our work explored drag as a form of expression, as we created a subversive replication of a sports commentary wering drag. We also documented an adventure through brunswick in drag, exploring the concept of masculinity and societal expectations of presentation.

These works were exhibited in Pride Pallette IDAHOBIT exhibition 2024 & 2025 & The RMIT Gradute Exhibtion 2024

P5.js Projects

A large portion of my work explores digital interactive media.

A key work is Omission of self awareness, displayed at RMIT open day 2025

We are all aware of the existence of surveillance in "developed" society. This looks like cameras, biorecognition and even body tracking. This poses a scary reality for those who seek to disrupt systems of oppression through protest and organised changemaming, as more than ever we are able to be tracked.

In other ways too we are able to be tracked, out mobile devices, popping up in the back of people's photos, and our data online. Privacy is suddenly only a construct.

However, what happens when surveillance becomes the spectacle.

Photography

Photography

Photography is the backbone of my practice. I have been a photographer since 2020 with a keen interest in capturing moments that seem unimportant. My photography practice privileges interpersonal and personal moments of expression, rather than documentation or experimentation. I use photography as a medium for exploring private and public recollection of experience.
